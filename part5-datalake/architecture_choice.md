The most appropriate architecture of a fast-growing food delivery startup would be a Data Lakehouse.
The reason is that the system must operate with a variety of data types, such as structured data, such as payment transactions, semi-structured data, such as GPS logs, and unstructured data, such as customer reviews and menu pictures.

To start with, Data Lakehouse integrates the versatility of a Data Lake and the power and design of a Data Warehouse.
It enables the startup to store raw data in its original form and at the same time query and perform analytics efficiently.
The traditional data warehouses do not fit well in this case as they have difficulties with unstructured data such as images and texts.

Second, scalability plays a major role in a startup that is growing.
A Data Lakehouse can be scaled horizontally to process more and more data without having to specify fixed schema definitions in advance. 
This would come in handy when dealing with continuous streams such as GPS logs and customer interactions.

Thirdly, it favors advanced analytics and machine learning. 
The Analytics of sentiment Customer reviews can be analyzed, delivery optimization can be performed with the help of GPS data, and menu recognition can be carried out with the help of images.
A Data Lakehouse allows all these applications in a single platform.

Hence, a Data Lakehouse is the best option in this case because it is flexible, scalable, and capable of supporting advanced analytics.
